Methodology

The editor score is a simple sum. Each category is scored from 0 up to its maximum points, then we add them together to get a final score out of 100. The sections inside each server card mirror these same categories, so the rubric and the review topics match one to one. For vanilla Hytale servers, scoring rewards small gameplay footprint, transparent rules, and fair monetization, so “vanilla-first” stays meaningful. Scores are refreshed when a listing is re-checked, so the score and the “last checked” date are designed to stay aligned. A high score is about clarity and restraint, not popularity.

FAQ

Definitions stay simple on purpose, so “vanilla” remains useful as a label and not just a marketing word. If you are comparing vanilla Hytale servers, focus on gameplay footprint and fairness, then verify rules and monetization before you join. The goal is that you can read a server card fast and understand what is preserved, what is moderated, and what is changed. If a listing is vague about rules or monetization, treat it as unverified until it is clarified.

What does “vanilla” mean here?

Vanilla-first means minimal gameplay footprint: no heavy custom progression, no pay-to-win, and rules you can verify quickly. The goal is a stable baseline that still feels like Hytale, not a different game.

Why only one featured server?

Because “featured” should be earned through clarity and restraint, not buying placement. The other slots are open for review.

Do you accept paid listings?

No pay-for-score. If you want a review, you provide verifiable info. The listing stays unverified until it’s clear.

How often do you re-check?

When a listing changes or when a re-check is scheduled. The “last checked” date is meant to track verification freshness.
